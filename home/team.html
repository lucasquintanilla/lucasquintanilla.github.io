Lucas Quintanilla

Lucas Quintanilla was born in 1994 in Buenos Aires, Argentina. From an early age, he displayed a strong curiosity for science, technology and artistic expressions.

His educational journey began at Saint Boniface Technological Institute. Graduated as a Electro-Mechanical Technician in 2011, he developed a solid understanding of technical concepts in the area of electromechanics, science and maths developing his problem-solving and analytical thinking capabilities.

Motivated by his passion for technology, he pursued a degree in Information Systems Engineering at the National Technological University of Argentina specializing in Computer Systems Design. While studying and in his early 18 years he started working as a Sales Representative for Maxdream, a national travel company, where he developed strong communication skills, negotiation techniques, and customer relations expertise. Following his passion for technology, he started to show his studies working at the British Hospital of Buenos Aires as a Computer Technician in the Technological Department, at the age of 21 years old. This role refined his troubleshooting abilities, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills in a critical and high-pressure environment. He fully embraced his passion for computer systems, beginning a career as a Software Engineer at his 23. During this time, he worked on innovative digital solutions. His strong analytical approach allowed him to excel in systems analysis and design.

Driven by his curiosity for innovation, Lucas also interiorises in solar energy and their applications as a sustainable energy source. His critical thinking and problem-solving mindset extend beyond technology—he enjoys questioning life and the universe, thinking outside the box, and seeing things from different perspectives. Motivated by the increase of crime in Argentina he started to work for a Security Company that supplies technology to the national government and national organizations, where he was instructed on high-end militar technologies. He became increasingly interested in emerging technologies, particularly artificial intelligence, recognizing its transformative potential.

Beyond technology, Lucas has always been deeply involved in the music scene. He studied electronic music production in 2012, having played guitar since the age of 12 and his passion for music led him to form a rock band at his 20. He also worked as a private guitar teacher. In search of new opportunities, Lucas moved to Dublin, Ireland, determined to advance his career in technology. Inspired by the city's creative and tech scene, he began envisioning a platform that would merge art and technology, fostering collaboration and innovation. “I believe Dublin is a prime example of a city where tech and art cultures converge.“ “In an era where technology predominates, human expressions are essential to maintaining a balance.” As the Founder & Director of Technology at CreativeClub, Lucas combines his expertise in technology, and creative innovation. His multidisciplinary background in technology, music, and engineering allows him to lead the digital and technical aspects of the platform, shaping it into a dynamic space for creators. With a vision that fuses art and technology, Lucas is committed to building a collaborative, forward-thinking community that empowers artists, musicians, and innovators.

Inês Matos

Inês Matos is a Portuguese music teacher, composer, and pianist, born in Macao and raised in Portugal. She began studying piano at eight years old and has been dedicated to music ever since.

She completed her Bachelor’s degree in Music Composition at the Lisbon School of Music (Escola Superior de Música de Lisboa), where she explored subjects such as orchestration, composition techniques, contemporary music, music history, and more. During this time, she also helped organise university concerts, assisting with planning, logistics, and coordination.

Inês later completed a Specialised Course in a Master’s in Music Education at the University of Aveiro, where she studied creative methodologies, pedagogy, psychology, inclusion, early years music, and music theory and ear-training approaches.

She has been active in the music scene through teaching, singing in choirs, studying choral conducting, and working as a music transcriber.

In recent years, she has also studied digital creativity and social media, taking part in a mentorship programme and additional training focused on branding, positioning, visual identity, design, storytelling, communication strategy, and building a cohesive online presence.

Alongside this, she has been expanding her holistic approach to music, taking a Music Wellness course, a meditation course, and participating in mantra-singing workshops.

With over ten years of experience, Inês has supported people of all ages and backgrounds, guiding them in music, creative projects, and collaborative initiatives. She enjoys developing new ideas, exploring different artistic and professional fields, and helping others build skills, confidence, and meaningful outcomes. Her approach is warm, adaptable, and focused on fostering environments where creativity, learning, and growth can thrive.
